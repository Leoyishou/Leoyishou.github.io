=== ANKI ===

=== ANKI ===

she didn’t pursue a master’s degree, she went straight to work.

He found a part-time job as a tour guide

=== ANKI ===

=== ANKI ===
My undergraduate degree is in urban and rural planning, so I have some understanding of this field.

=== ANKI ===

=== ANKI ===
It seems not

=== ANKI ===
It kind of depends

它不会过期吗?
=== ANKI ===
Won’t it expire?

Your phrase “Will it not expire?” is grammatically correct, but it might sound a bit formal or old-fashioned. In most contexts, people would say “Won’t it expire?” which is a more common contraction of “Will it not”.

“会员卡是和你的身份证绑定的吗?”
=== ANKI ===
“Is this membership bound to your ID card?”

它没有一个固定的名字
=== ANKI ===
It doesn’t have a fixed name

所以他们有计划去设置一些国家公园
=== ANKI ===
So they have plans to establish some national parks.

在美国，它们有自己的名字。
=== ANKI ===
The ones in the US have a name.

怎么说呢
=== ANKI ===
“How do I say” is typically used in situations where you are considering your own ability to express something. If you’re considering the listener’s ability to understand or accept what you’re saying, “How should I put it” might be a better choice.”

你不认为你会养宠物
=== ANKI ===
You don’t think you’ll ever get a pet

这只兔子被我养的非常胖”
=== ANKI ===
“It was raised by me to be very fat” or “I raised it to be very fat.” The sentence “It was growed by me very fat” is not correct English. In English, we would say “It was raised by me to be very fat” or “I raised it to be very fat.” The verb “grow” is not typically used with animals in English; instead, we use the verb “raise.” Also, the word “growed” is not correct English; the past tense of “grow” is “grew,” and the past participle is “grown.”

=== ANKI ===
they are not kept in cages, but have a larger space to move around.

可能是我太久没看到野生动物了
=== ANKI ===
