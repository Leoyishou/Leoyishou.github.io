What’s the difference between brutal and cruel

“Brutal” and “cruel” are two English words that both describe actions or behaviors that cause harm or suffering. They do have slightly different connotations, however:

  1. “Brutal”: This adjective is used to describe something that is exceedingly savage, violent, or harsh. It often implies a lack of human sensibility or a disregard for the pain inflicted. For example, a “brutal” beating would suggest a high level of physical violence.

  2. “Cruel”: This adjective is used to describe someone or something that deliberately causes pain or suffering. This can be either physical or emotional suffering. Cruelty often involves a certain level of intentionality or pleasure in causing suffering. For instance, a “cruel” person might be someone who taunts or mocks others to cause emotional pain.

The main difference between these two terms is the emphasis on physicality and severity in “brutal” and the emphasis on intentionality and emotional pain in “cruel”. That being said, these terms are often used interchangeably in many contexts.
